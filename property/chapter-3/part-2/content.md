# Part 2: Commercial Property

## Overview
Commercial property insurance protects businesses from financial losses due to damage to their buildings, equipment, inventory, and other business assets. Unlike personal lines, commercial policies are more complex and customizable.

## Section 1: Commercial Property Conditions

### Loss Conditions
1. **Abandonment** - Insured cannot abandon property to insurer
2. **Appraisal** - Either party can demand appraisal of loss amount (not coverage)
3. **Duties After Loss** - Notify police (theft), give prompt notice, protect property, cooperate, submit sworn proof of loss
4. **Loss Payment** - Insurer has options: pay value, repair/replace, take property at agreed value
5. **Recovered Property** - Either party can recover stolen property; insured must return payment or keep property
6. **Vacancy** - After 60 consecutive days vacant, coverage suspended for vandalism, sprinkler leakage, building glass breakage, water damage, theft; other covered perils pay only 85%
7. **Valuation** - ACV unless stated otherwise; special valuation for tenant improvements, stock sold but not delivered

### General Conditions
1. **Concealment, Misrepresentation, Fraud** - Voids coverage if intentional
2. **Control of Property** - Acts/neglect of non-insured persons don't affect coverage
3. **Legal Action** - 1 year after loss to sue insurer
4. **Liberalization** - If ISO broadens coverage without premium increase, existing policies automatically get the broadened coverage
5. **No Benefit to Bailee** - Policy won't benefit anyone holding insured's property
6. **Other Insurance** - If other valid insurance covers same loss, pays proportionately
7. **Policy Period/Coverage Territory** - Covered only during policy period in coverage territory
8. **Transfer of Rights** - Rights transfer to insurer after payment (subrogation); insured must assist

## Section 2: Building and Personal Property Coverage Form (BPP)

### What's Covered
**Building Coverage:**
- Building/structure described in declarations
- Completed additions
- Permanently installed fixtures, machinery, equipment
- Outdoor fixtures
- Personal property owned by insured used to maintain building

**Personal Property Coverage:**
- Furniture and fixtures
- Machinery and equipment
- Stock (inventory)
- All other personal property owned by insured used in business
- Labor, materials, services on personal property
- Tenant improvements (if tenant)
- Leased personal property insured is contractually responsible for

### What's NOT Covered
- Accounts, bills, currency, money, securities (except $2,500 for theft)
- Animals unless owned, boarded, held for sale
- Automobiles held for sale
- Bridges, roadways, patios, parking lots
- Contraband
- Cost of excavations, grading, backfilling
- Foundations below lowest basement floor
- Land, water, growing crops
- Personal property while airborne or waterborne
- Pilings, piers, wharves, docks
- Property more specifically insured
- Retaining walls not part of building
- Underground pipes, flues, drains
- Vehicles or self-propelled machines (licensed for road use)
- Grain, hay, straw, fertilizer in open

### Coverage Extensions (Additional Insurance)
Only apply if 80% or more coinsurance OR value reporting:
1. **Newly Acquired Property** - $250,000 for buildings, $100,000 for personal property; 30 days coverage
2. **Personal Effects** - $2,500 per person
3. **Valuable Papers** - $2,500 for research costs
4. **Property Off-Premises** - $10,000 while temporarily off-premises
5. **Outdoor Property** - $1,000 total, $250 per item (fences, signs, trees, plants); only for specific perils

### Additional Coverages (Part of Limits)
1. **Debris Removal** - 25% of loss plus $25,000 if limit exhausted
2. **Preservation of Property** - 30 days at another location
3. **Fire Department Service Charge** - $1,000 max
4. **Pollutant Cleanup** - $10,000 per location per year
5. **Increased Cost of Construction** - $10,000 max
6. **Electronic Data** - $2,500 (for cost to restore lost data)

## Section 3: Causes of Loss Forms

### Basic Form - Named Perils
Fire, Lightning, Explosion, Windstorm/Hail, Smoke, Aircraft/Vehicles, Riot/Civil Commotion, Vandalism, Sprinkler Leakage, Sinkhole Collapse, Volcanic Action

### Broad Form - Named Perils
All Basic perils PLUS:
- Falling objects
- Weight of snow, ice, sleet
- Water damage (from appliances, heating, AC, fire protective systems, hot water heaters)

AND these Business Income/Extra Expense triggers:
- Breakage of building glass
- Collapse from specific causes

### Special Form - Open Perils (most comprehensive)
All risks EXCEPT those specifically excluded.

**Key Exclusions in Special Form:**
- Ordinance or law
- Earth movement
- Government action
- Nuclear hazard
- War
- Water (flood, surface water, overflow)
- Fungus, wet/dry rot, bacteria
- Utility services failure
- Mechanical breakdown
- Neglect
- Weather conditions to personal property in open
- Acts or decisions (planning, design, specifications)
- Faulty workmanship
- Collapse (except if caused by covered perils)

## Section 4: Business Income Coverage

### What It Covers
- Net Income (profit) that would have been earned
- Continuing normal operating expenses, including payroll

### Key Terms
- **Period of Restoration** - Begins 72 hours after direct physical loss; ends when property should be repaired with reasonable speed
- **Business Income** - Net Income + Continuing Operating Expenses (including payroll)
- **Coinsurance Options** - 50%, 60%, 70%, 80%, 90%, 100%, 125%

### Additional Coverages
1. **Civil Authority** - Coverage when government prohibits access; 3-week maximum; must be within 1 mile of covered property
2. **Alterations and New Buildings** - Covers income loss during construction
3. **Extended Business Income** - Continues 60 days after operations resume (for gradual return of customers)

## Section 5: Extra Expense Coverage

### What It Covers
Expenses to continue operations that you wouldn't have had without the loss:
- Temporary location rental
- Moving costs
- Emergency equipment rental
- Expediting repairs

### Key Points
- Covers expenses above normal operating costs
- Goal: Minimize or avoid suspension of operations
- Can be combined with Business Income or standalone

## Section 6: Commercial Package Policy (CPP)

### Components
A CPP bundles multiple coverages:
- Commercial Property
- Commercial General Liability
- Commercial Crime
- Commercial Inland Marine
- Equipment Breakdown
- Farm Coverage

### Minimum Requirements
Must include at least TWO coverages from different categories

### Advantages
- Single policy, single premium
- Coordinated coverage
- Usually less expensive than separate policies
- Easier to manage

## Section 7: Equipment Breakdown Coverage

### Formerly Called
Boiler and Machinery Insurance

### What It Covers
Sudden and accidental breakdown of covered equipment:
- Boilers and pressure vessels
- Air conditioning/refrigeration
- Electrical equipment
- Mechanical equipment
- Production machinery
- Computer equipment

### Key Terms
- **Breakdown** - Must be sudden and accidental; does NOT include gradual deterioration
- **Object** - The specific equipment covered

### What's NOT Covered
- Wear and tear, deterioration, rust, corrosion
- Depletion, deterioration of operating supplies
- Testing or startup procedures
- Any breakdown specifically excluded

### Additional Coverages Often Included
- Spoilage coverage (for perishables)
- Utility interruption
- Hazardous substance coverage
- Data restoration
- Ordinance or law (increased cost to rebuild to code)

## Section 8: Businessowners Policy (BOP)

### Overview
A package policy designed for small-to-medium businesses. Combines property and liability in one policy.

### Eligibility
- Specific business classes (offices, retail, apartments, restaurants)
- Size limitations (varies by class)
- NOT eligible: Auto dealers, places of amusement, bars, banks, contractors

### Property Coverage Included
- Building (if owned)
- Business Personal Property
- Business Income with Extra Expense (includes Extended Business Income)
- Tenants improvements
- Outdoor property

### Key Features
- **Blanket Coverage** - One limit applies to building and contents combined
- **Replacement Cost** - Standard (not ACV)
- **No Coinsurance** - Seasonal increases in limits at no extra charge
- **Off-Premises Coverage** - Included
- **Debris Removal** - Included
- **Valuable Papers** - $25,000 included
- **Electronic Data** - Included

### Causes of Loss
- **Standard BOP** - Broad Form perils (named perils)
- **Special Form BOP** - Open perils for building/BPP, named perils for personal property of others

### Business Income in BOP
- **12-month limit** on coverage
- **Extended Business Income** - 60 days
- **Civil Authority** - 30 days (longer than standard commercial)

## Section 10: Exam Trap Alerts

1. **Vacancy vs Unoccupancy** - Vacant = no contents or people; Unoccupied = contents present but no people. Only VACANCY triggers reduction.

2. **60 Days Vacant** - Suspends VMM, sprinkler, glass, water, theft; reduces other perils to 85%.

3. **72-Hour Wait** - Business Income coverage doesn't start until 72 hours after loss.

4. **Additional vs Extension** - Additional coverage = part of your limit; Coverage Extensions = additional insurance.

5. **Civil Authority Distance** - Must be within 1 MILE of covered property.

6. **BOP Coinsurance** - There is NO coinsurance in a BOP! Don't be tricked.

7. **Special Form** - Open perils for buildings/BPP, but NAMED perils for personal property of others.

8. **Equipment Breakdown** - NOT covered by regular property forms; requires separate coverage.

9. **Appraisal** - Only for AMOUNT of loss, not whether something is covered.

10. **Liberalization** - Automatic if ISO broadens coverage without premium increase.
