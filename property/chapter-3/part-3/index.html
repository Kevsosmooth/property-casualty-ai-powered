1. What is Inland Marine Insurance?

Despite the name, Inland Marine insurance has nothing to do with water. It evolved from ocean marine insurance to cover property that is mobile, in transit over land, or has special characteristics unsuitable for standard property policies.

Property in Transit

Being transported from place to place

Mobile Property

Designed to move frequently

Bailee Property

Held by someone other than owner

Infrastructure

Bridges, tunnels, towers, pipelines

Real-World Scenario: Why Inland Marine?

The Setup: Sarah is a photographer with $50,000 in camera equipment. She works at weddings, outdoor locations, and her studio.

The Problem: Her Homeowners policy only covers property AT her home with limited theft coverage.

The Solution: A Personal Articles Floater covers her cameras WORLDWIDE - at home, in transit, or at shooting locations. Coverage "floats" with the property.

2. Nationwide Marine Definition (NAIC)

The Nationwide Marine Definition (NAIC, 1953, revised 1976) determines what may properly be classified as Inland Marine. It defines 4 general classes of risks:

1. Domestic Shipments & Transportation

Property being shipped within the country - cargo, goods in transit

2. Instrumentalities of Transportation/Communication

Bridges, tunnels, piers, wharves, docks, pipelines, radio/TV towers

3. Commercial Property Floater Risks

Business property that moves - contractor equipment, bailee property

4. Personal Property Floater Risks

Individual valuables - jewelry, cameras, musical instruments, fine arts

3. Personal Floaters

A personal floater covers movable personal property wherever located. Called "floaters" because coverage floats with the property. May be written on all-risk (open peril) or named-peril basis.

Floater Type Coverage Basis Key Features Limits
Personal Property Floater All-risk, UNSCHEDULED Blanket limit covers everything worldwide Special limits on certain categories
Personal Articles Floater All-risk, SCHEDULED Items listed with agreed values, worldwide, usually NO deductible Pays scheduled amount
Personal Effects Floater All-risk, TRAVELERS For items carried/worn during travel, NOT at home $100 limit jewelry/watches/furs

Personal Articles Floater (PAF) - Most Common

Pre-Printed Categories:

Jewelry Furs Cameras Musical Instruments Fine Arts Silverware

Key Benefits:

  • - Open perils (all-risk) coverage
  • - Worldwide coverage
  • - Agreed value (no depreciation)
  • - Usually no deductible

Example: Jennifer's $15,000 ring is stolen in Paris. Her HO policy pays only $1,500 (sublimit). With a PAF scheduled at $15,000, she gets full value, no deductible, no depreciation - even overseas.

Personal Effects Floater - Critical Limitation!

Coverage is for travelers ONLY - NOT while property is at home. Jewelry, watches, and furs limited to $100. NO coverage for vehicles, bicycles, currency, or travel tickets.

4. Commercial Inland Marine Conditions

These conditions are in addition to common policy conditions. Key conditions include:

Abandonment

Insured cannot abandon property to insurer

Other Insurance

Policy is EXCESS over other insurance

Pair, Set or Parts

Pays difference in value, not total set

Privilege to Adjust with Owner

Insurer settles with actual property owners

Reinstatement of Limit

Claims don't reduce limit (except total loss on scheduled item)

Subrogation

Transfer rights of recovery to insurer

Valuation Condition

Value determined by the LESSER OF:

ACV

Actual Cash Value

Restore

Reasonable Cost to Restore

Replace

Cost to Replace with Like Kind

5. Filed vs Unfiled Forms

FILED Forms

  • - Standardized, filed with regulators
  • - Usually $250 deductible
  • - Usually open peril basis
  • - Commercial Property Causes of Loss forms NOT applicable

UNFILED Forms

  • - Custom forms for unique exposures
  • - May not require regulatory approval
  • - Greater flexibility
  • - Regulations vary by state

6. Key Commercial Coverage Forms

Accounts Receivable (FILED)

Covers losses of amounts due from customers that become uncollectable because A/R records are damaged/destroyed.

Note: Unlike most IM, covers property at FIXED location. Must store records in described receptacle.

Valuable Papers & Records (FILED)

Covers cost to reconstruct documents - manuscripts, deeds, drawings, maps, mortgages.

Note: NO coinsurance. Money, securities, and data NOT covered.

Contractors Equipment (UNFILED)

HIGHEST PREMIUM of all commercial IM. Covers mobile equipment at job sites and in transit.

Auto coverage: 60 days for new equipment, up to 25% of limit or $50,000.

Jewelers Block (FILED)

Covers dealer's inventory AND customer property in care. Optional transit coverage available.

Note: NOT covered while worn by insured/employees or at trade shows.

Bailee's Customer (UNFILED)

Covers customer property in insured's care - dry cleaners, furriers, repair shops, warehouses.

Warehouseman's: Usually written as EXCESS over owner's insurance.

Signs (FILED)

Covers neon, fluorescent, automatic, mechanical signs. Commercial property only covers $2,500 for outdoor signs.

100% COINSURANCE required!

Real-World Scenario: Bailee Coverage

Setup: Elite Dry Cleaners has 200 customer garments ($150 avg each). A fire destroys everything. Result: Bailee coverage pays $30,000 for customer property. The "Privilege to Adjust with Owner" condition lets insurer settle directly with customers.

7. Transportation Coverage

Common Carriers

Transport for ANYONE. STRICT liability (unless Acts of God, public enemy, shipper fault, inherent vice).

Contract Carriers

Written contracts only. Liable only if NEGLIGENT.

Private Carriers

Transport their OWN property only.

F.O.B. (Free On Board) - Who Bears Transit Risk?

F.O.B. Shipper's Location

CONSIGNEE (receiver) owns and bears risk once carrier takes possession.

F.O.B. Destination (Consignee's Location)

SHIPPER owns and bears risk until delivery to consignee.

Regular Bill of Lading

Carrier responsible for invoice cost. No value declaration required.

Released Bill of Lading

Shipper declares value. Carrier liable only up to stated amount. Lower shipping fees.

Motor Truck Cargo - Carriers Form

For common carriers. Legal liability coverage. Covered until delivery, up to 72 hours at unspecified location.

Motor Truck Cargo - Owners Form

For private carriers shipping own goods. Direct damage coverage. Liability not a factor.

Real-World Scenario: F.O.B. Loss

Setup: Factory ships $100K furniture to retailer, F.O.B. Factory. Truck accident destroys $40K of goods. Who bears loss? The RETAILER (consignee) - because F.O.B. Factory means ownership transferred when goods left factory. Retailer needs their own transit coverage.

Exam Trap Alerts

1. "Marine" ≠ Water

Inland Marine = LAND transit/mobile property. Ocean Marine = water.

2. PAF = Agreed Value

Personal Articles Floater pays scheduled amount. No depreciation.

3. Personal Effects = $100 Jewelry Limit

Only $100 for jewelry/watches/furs. NOT covered at home.

4. Common vs Contract Carrier

Common = STRICT liability. Contract = only if NEGLIGENT.

5. F.O.B. Shipper's = Consignee Risk

F.O.B. Shipper = RECEIVER bears transit risk.

6. Motor Truck Cargo ≠ The Truck

Covers the CARGO being transported, not the vehicle (that's auto).

7. Other Insurance = EXCESS

IM policy pays AFTER other insurance covering same loss.

8. A/R = Fixed Location Exception

Accounts Receivable covers property at FIXED location (unlike most IM).

9. Signs = 100% Coinsurance

Signs form requires 100% coinsurance (most others use 80%).

10. Reinstatement of Limit

Claims don't reduce limit EXCEPT total loss on scheduled item.
