1. What is Inland Marine Insurance?

Despite the name, Inland Marine insurance has nothing to do with water. It evolved from ocean marine insurance to cover property that is mobile, in transit over land, or has special characteristics unsuitable for standard property policies.

What Makes Property "Inland Marine"?

Property in Transit

Being transported from place to place

Mobile Property

Designed to move frequently

Bailee Property

Held by someone other than owner

Infrastructure

Bridges, tunnels, towers, pipelines

Why Standard Property Policies Don't Work

Standard property policies cover property at a fixed location. But what about a photographer's cameras used at weddings, job sites, and travel? Or a contractor's bulldozer that moves between projects? These need coverage that follows the property - that's Inland Marine.

Real-World Scenario: Why Inland Marine?

The Setup: Sarah is a professional photographer with $50,000 in camera equipment. She works at weddings, outdoor locations, corporate events, and her home studio.

The Problem: Her Homeowners policy only covers property AT her home. Even then, business equipment has limited coverage ($2,500 typical) and theft away from home is restricted.

The Solution: A Personal Articles Floater covers her cameras WORLDWIDE - at home, in her car, at a destination wedding in Italy. Coverage "floats" with the property wherever she takes it.

2. Nationwide Marine Definition (NAIC)

The Nationwide Marine Definition was established by the NAIC in 1953 (revised 1976) to determine what may properly be classified as Inland Marine insurance. It prevents insurers from using "marine" classification to avoid rate regulations.

The 4 General Classes of Inland Marine Risks

1. Domestic Shipments & Transportation

Property being shipped within the country - cargo, goods in transit by truck, rail, or air

Example: A furniture manufacturer ships 50 sofas from their NJ factory to a retailer in California. Motor Truck Cargo insurance covers the shipment.

2. Instrumentalities of Transportation/Communication

Infrastructure that enables transportation - bridges, tunnels, piers, wharves, docks, pipelines, radio/TV towers

Example: A cell phone company insures its transmission towers across multiple states under Inland Marine coverage.

3. Commercial Property Floater Risks

Business property that moves - contractor equipment, bailee property, salesperson samples

Example: A construction company's bulldozers, cranes, and excavators that move between job sites need a Contractors Equipment Floater.

4. Personal Property Floater Risks

Individual valuables - jewelry, cameras, musical instruments, fine arts, furs

Example: A concert violinist insures her $200,000 Stradivarius with a Personal Articles Floater - covered at home, on tour, everywhere.

Memory Trick: "DICP"

Domestic Shipments, Instrumentalities, Commercial Floaters, Personal Floaters

3. Personal Floaters

A personal floater covers movable personal property wherever located. Called "floaters" because coverage floats with the property - not tied to one location. May be written on all-risk (open peril) or named-peril basis.

The 3 Types of Personal Floaters

Floater Type Coverage Basis Key Features Limits
Personal Property Floater All-risk, UNSCHEDULED Blanket limit covers everything worldwide Special limits on certain categories
Personal Articles Floater (PAF) All-risk, SCHEDULED Items listed with agreed values, worldwide, usually NO deductible Pays scheduled amount (agreed value)
Personal Effects Floater All-risk, TRAVELERS For items carried/worn during travel, NOT at home $100 limit jewelry/watches/furs

Personal Articles Floater (PAF) - The Most Common

Pre-Printed Categories:

Jewelry Furs Cameras Musical Instruments Fine Arts Silverware Golfer's Equipment

Key Benefits:

  • - Open perils (all-risk) coverage
  • - Worldwide coverage
  • - Agreed value (no depreciation)
  • - Usually NO deductible
  • - No coinsurance requirement

Real-World Scenario: PAF Saves the Day

The Setup: Jennifer has a $15,000 engagement ring scheduled on her PAF at $15,000 agreed value.

What Happens: The ring is stolen while she's on vacation in Paris.

The Result: PAF pays the full $15,000 - no depreciation, no deductible, no questions about "actual cash value." Her HO policy would have only paid $1,500 (standard sublimit for jewelry theft).

Personal Property Floater (Unscheduled)

Provides blanket coverage for personal property without listing each item. Good for people with many valuable items who don't want to schedule each one.

Real-World Scenario: Blanket Coverage

The Setup: Mark is a collector with hundreds of items - vintage watches, rare books, antique coins.

The Solution: A Personal Property Floater with a $100,000 blanket limit covers everything without listing each item. Special sublimits still apply to certain categories.

Personal Effects Floater - CRITICAL LIMITATIONS!

What It Covers:

  • - Personal effects while TRAVELING
  • - Items carried or worn away from home

Critical Limitations:

  • - NOT covered while at home
  • - $100 limit on jewelry, watches, furs
  • - NO coverage for vehicles, bicycles
  • - NO coverage for currency, tickets

Real-World Scenario: The $100 Trap

The Setup: Tom has a Personal Effects Floater and takes his $5,000 Rolex on vacation.

What Happens: The watch is stolen from his hotel room in Miami.

The Result: Personal Effects Floater pays only $100 (the jewelry/watch limit). Tom should have used a PAF with the watch scheduled!

Exam Trap Alerts

1. "Marine" = Water?

NO! Inland Marine = LAND transit and mobile property. Ocean Marine = water. Don't confuse them!

2. PAF = Agreed Value

Personal Articles Floater pays the SCHEDULED amount - no depreciation, no ACV calculation.

3. Personal Effects = $100 Jewelry

Only $100 for jewelry/watches/furs. AND it's NOT covered while at home - travel only!

4. Scheduled vs Unscheduled

PAF = Scheduled (items listed). Personal Property Floater = Unscheduled (blanket).

5. "Floater" Meaning

Coverage "floats" with property wherever it goes. Not tied to one location like standard property.

6. NAIC Definition Purpose

Determines what CAN be classified as Inland Marine - prevents rate regulation avoidance.
