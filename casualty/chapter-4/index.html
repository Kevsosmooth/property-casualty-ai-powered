Auto Insurance Coverage, Definitions & Garage Coverage

The Big Picture: What Auto Insurance Covers

When you own or drive a car, you face 3 types of potential loss. Auto insurance is designed to protect you from ALL of them:

1

Legal Liability

You hurt someone or damage their property - you owe them money

Covered by: Part A

2

Injury to You/Family

You or your family get hurt in an accident

Covered by: Part B & C

3

Damage to Your Car

Your car gets damaged or stolen

Covered by: Part D

PAP Structure: The 6 Parts

Think of it like this:

The PAP is like a house with 6 rooms. Each room has its own purpose, and you need to know what each room does!

Part A: Liability

Pays when YOU cause an accident and hurt someone or damage their stuff

Part B: Medical Payments

Pays medical bills for you and passengers - no matter who's at fault

Part C: UM/UIM

Protects you when the OTHER driver has no insurance or not enough

Part D: Physical Damage

Pays to fix or replace YOUR car (Collision + Comprehensive)

Part E: Duties After Loss

What you MUST do after an accident to keep your coverage

Part F: General Provisions

The rules that apply to the whole policy
