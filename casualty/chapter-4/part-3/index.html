1. Types of Licensees

Lines of Authority

A person cannot sell, solicit, or negotiate insurance unless licensed for the appropriate line(s):

Major Lines:

  • •Life
  • •Accident and Health or Sickness
  • •Variable Life and Variable Annuity*
  • •Property
  • •Casualty
  • •Personal Lines
  • •Surplus Lines
  • •Title Insurance

*Requires securities license too!

Limited Lines:

  • •Bail bonds
  • •Car rental insurance
  • •Credit insurance
  • •Ticket insurance
  • •Travel insurance
  • •Group mortgage cancellation
  • •Legal insurance
  • •Self-storage personal property

Producer License Requirements

1

Be 18 years of age or older

2

Submit NAIC uniform application + fees

3

Complete prelicensing education (unless exempt)

4

Pass exam within 1 year of application

5

Be NJ resident OR plan to work at NJ office

6

No controlled business (not just for self/family)

Example: Getting licensed just to write your own policies or your spouse's business = NOT allowed

7

Good reputation, no crimes of moral turpitude

Examples: Fraud, theft, forgery, perjury = moral turpitude. Traffic tickets = NOT moral turpitude

8

Submit fingerprints + background check consent

Types of Producer Licenses

Resident Producer

Individual domiciled in NJ or working at NJ office

Must pass NJ exam & complete prelicensing

Business Entity

Corporation, LLC, partnership licensed as producer

Must have at least 1 licensed partner/officer

Nonresident Producer

Licensed in another state, wants to transact in NJ

Must appoint Commissioner as agent for legal process

Example: A PA-licensed agent with NJ clients can get a NJ nonresident license

Surplus Lines Producer

Writes business for unauthorized insurers

Must be NJ resident + hold P&C license

Example: Client needs coverage no NJ-admitted insurer will write (high-risk property) → surplus lines producer places it with Lloyd's of London

Business Entity Ban:

A producer license will NOT be issued to a business entity operated by a person who had their license revoked within the past 10 years.

2. Obtaining a License

Prelicensing Education

20 Hours

Per line of authority

Must include NJ regulations

Self-study & online courses count

After completing prelicensing, must pass state exam.

Professional Designation Waivers

Commissioner may waive prelicensing if applicant holds approved designation:

For Life, A&H, Variable:

CLU ChFC CFP FLMI LUTCF CIC RHU CEBS REBC HIA

For Property, Casualty, Surplus:

CPCU AAI ARM CIC

Temporary Work Authority

60 Days

Maximum duration

After completing all required procedures, applicant can begin working immediately with temporary work authority while waiting for actual license.

Expires when actual license is issued or after 60 days (whichever is first)

Who Does NOT Need a License?

✓ Executive, admin, managerial, or clerical employees of insurers
✓ Special agent employees providing technical advice
✓ Persons who only secure info for group insurance
✓ Employers administering employee benefits programs
✓ Employees doing inspection, rating, or training
✓ Persons whose activities limited to advertising
✓ Nonresidents selling multi-state commercial P&C
✓ Salaried employees advising employer on insurance

Key: Not directly involved in sales + no commission received

4. License Refusal and Termination

Cancellation

Voluntary termination by licensee

Return license to Department

Cannot avoid disciplinary hearing!

Suspension

Temporary loss of license

Can reapply immediately after suspension ends

Revocation

Permanent loss of license

Must wait 5 YEARS to reapply!

Grounds for Revocation or Suspension

✗ Willfully violating Insurance Code
✗ Withholding material information
✗ Misrepresentation or fraud
✗ Cheating on examination
✗ License revoked in another state
✗ Misappropriating funds
✗ Misrepresenting policy conditions
✗ Paying rebates to induce business
✗ Crime of moral turpitude
✗ Failing to notify Commissioner of conviction within 30 days
✗ Forgery on insurance documents
✗ Accepting business from unlicensed producer
✗ Failing to pay child support
✗ Failing to pay state income tax

Important Rules:

  • • All licenses are property of the State of NJ - must be returned upon suspension/revocation
  • • Person with revoked license cannot work in any capacity with a licensed producer
  • • After revocation, must submit affidavit describing how character has improved during the 5 years
  • • Surplus lines: Failure to maintain bond = void authority

Company Appointing Producer

1

15 Days
2

Insurer notifies Commissioner of appointment

Agency Contract Must Specify:

  • • Lines of authority the producer may represent
  • • Duties, responsibilities, and limitations

Insurer cannot accept business produced before individual was licensed!

Termination of Agency Contract

15 Days

To notify Commissioner

If insurer cancels agency contract, must notify Commissioner in writing. Failure implies contract still exists.

Temporary License (Death/Disability)

180 Days

Maximum

No examination required for temporary license in special circumstances.

May Be Issued To:

  • • Death/disability: Surviving spouse, personal representative, or business entity member
  • • Military service: Designee of the licensed agent
  • • Public interest: Any person when Commissioner deems necessary

Commissioner may require temporary licensee to have a licensed sponsor.

Exam Trap Alerts

!

Variable products: Require BOTH insurance license AND securities license.

!

Surplus lines: Must be NJ RESIDENT + hold P&C license.

!

24 CE hours: 3 must be in ETHICS - not optional!

!

Canceling to avoid discipline: Doesn't work - disciplinary action continues.

!

15 days: Both for appointment notice AND termination notice to Commissioner.

!

After revocation: Cannot work in ANY capacity with licensed producer for 5 years.
