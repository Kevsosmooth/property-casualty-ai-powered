Coverage A: Bodily Injury & Property Damage Liability

What Coverage A Protects Against

Coverage A is the core of CGL - it provides coverage for third-party bodily injury and property damage claims arising from your business operations.

Bodily Injury (BI)

Provides coverage for injury, sickness, disease, or death suffered by third parties because of the insured's business activities.

Examples:

  • Customer slips on wet floor in your store
  • Visitor injured by falling merchandise
  • Person hurt by equipment at your location

Property Damage (PD)

Provides coverage for injury to, destruction of, or loss of use of property of others due to the insured's business activities.

Examples:

  • Your employee damages customer's car
  • Your product causes damage to someone's home
  • Your work destroys client's property

Insuring Agreement Requirement

For Coverage A to apply, the injury or damage must:

  • Occur during the policy period
  • Take place within the coverage territory
  • Be caused by an "occurrence" (accident)

Claims-Made Extended Reporting Periods (Tails)

Critical for Claims-Made Policies!

These "tails" only apply to Claims-Made policies. They provide ways to extend your reporting period after your policy ends.

60 Days

Mini-Tail (Automatically Included)

You have 60 days after your policy expires to report any incident that occurred during your policy period. If you report it within 60 days, you're covered.

Key Point:

If an accident happens within 60 days following the expiration of the policy, the policy will still cover it.

5 Years

Midi-Tail / Basic Extended Reporting Period (Automatically Included)

If you report a potential problem within the 60-day mini-tail, the insurer will cover any lawsuit related to that problem for up to 5 years from the date of the original incident.

How It Works:

As long as the insurer is notified about a potential claim within 60 days after the policy expires, coverage will apply for claims filed within 5 years of the incident.

Unlimited

Supplemental ERP / Full Tail (Must Purchase - 200% of Premium)

To get additional coverage after policy ends, you can purchase the Supplemental Extended Reporting Period Endorsement. It essentially converts the claims-made form to an occurrence-type coverage by conversion.

Cost:

Usually 200% of the annual premium

Example: $5,000 annual premium = $10,000 for full tail

Coverage:

Unlimited time to report claims for incidents during policy period

Important Limitation:

Subject to the policy's aggregate limit for the life of the period. The aggregate NEVER renews - once exhausted, no more coverage.

All Three Tails in Action - Complete Example

Scenario Setup

You're a contractor with a $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate claims-made policy.

Retroactive Date:

January 1, 2020 (your first claims-made policy date)

Current Policy Period:

January 1, 2024 - December 31, 2024

Policy Expires:

December 31, 2024 (you don't renew)

Incident:

Bathroom renovation completed June 15, 2024

Scenario 1: Mini-Tail Success

February 1, 2025: Homeowner notices water damage and sues you

(37 days after expiration)

COVERED

Reported within 60-day mini-tail

Scenario 2: Midi-Tail Success

February 15, 2025: You notice small crack, report it to insurer (46 days after expiration - within mini-tail)

July 2027: Homeowner finally sues you (2.5 years after the incident)

COVERED

Reported within mini-tail, sued within 5 years of incident

Scenario 3: No Coverage - Mini-Tail Expired

March 15, 2025: Homeowner discovers damage and sues you

(74 days after expiration)

NOT COVERED

Not reported within 60-day mini-tail, no supplemental ERP purchased

Scenario 4: Supplemental ERP Success

You paid 200% of premium ($10,000 if annual premium was $5,000) for lifetime coverage

June 2030: Homeowner sues you (6 years after incident)

COVERED

Supplemental ERP covers claims forever, as long as incident occurred during 2024 policy period. Your $2M aggregate is reduced by whatever this claim costs.

Scenario 5: Aggregate Exhausted

You bought the supplemental ERP

Previous lawsuits from 2024 incidents have paid out $2M total

2032: Another lawsuit from a 2024 incident

NOT COVERED

Aggregate limit exhausted, even though you have unlimited time coverage
