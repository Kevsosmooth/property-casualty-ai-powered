# New Jersey Life Insurance Exam

## About the Exam

Before you take your state exam, you need to prepare yourself not only in terms of studying the material, but also in terms of becoming familiar with test-taking procedures and environment.

A Candidate Handbook or License Information Bulletin for your state provides invaluable information about specific testing procedures, including how to schedule or reschedule your examination, the types of identification you will need, how early to arrive at the testing center, and what you will not be allowed to bring into the testing center. Reading this is an integral part of preparing for your examination. That information may be found on the website of the testing provider in your state or through the insurance department.

## Exam Breakdown

Use the Exam Breakdown to know where to focus your valuable study time. Each exam has a certain number of questions allocated to each chapter, so concentrating your studies on the chapters that are more heavily weighted on the exam will benefit you when taking the actual exam.

**New Jersey Life Insurance**
- 83 Questions
- Time Limit: 3.5 hours
- Passing Score: 70%

### Chapter Weighting

**Chapter 1: Completing the Application, Underwriting, and Delivering the Policy** - 14%

**Chapter 2: Types of Policies** - 11%

**Chapter 3: Policy Riders, Provisions, Options, and Exclusions** - 25%

**Chapter 4: Taxes, Retirement, and Other Insurance Concepts** - 10%

**Chapter 5: New Jersey Laws, Rules, and Regulations Common to All Lines** - 30%

**Chapter 6: New Jersey Laws, Rules, and Regulations Pertinent to Life Insurance Only** - 10%

### Study Priority (by weight)

1. Chapter 5 - NJ Laws Common to All Lines (30%) -- HEAVIEST
2. Chapter 3 - Policy Riders, Provisions, Options, and Exclusions (25%)
3. Chapter 1 - Completing the Application, Underwriting, and Delivering the Policy (14%)
4. Chapter 2 - Types of Policies (11%)
5. Chapter 4 - Taxes, Retirement, and Other Insurance Concepts (10%)
6. Chapter 6 - NJ Laws Pertinent to Life Only (10%)

Note that on your state exam, you may also be asked additional 5-19 pretest questions that will not be scored, and the time taken to answer them will not count against examination time.
