## How to Structure Your Markdown

Use these exact headers so I know how to split the content into pages:

```
# CHAPTER 1 - Field Underwriting Procedures

## CHAPTER 1 - INTRODUCTION
(paste the intro/overview content here)

## CHAPTER 1 - PART 1: (Topic Name)
(paste part 1 content here)

## CHAPTER 1 - PART 2: (Topic Name)
(paste part 2 content here)

## CHAPTER 1 - PART 3: (Topic Name)
(paste part 3 content here)

...continue for all parts...

## CHAPTER 1 - RECAP
(paste recap/summary content here)
```

**Important:**
- Include the topic name after the part number when you can (e.g., "PART 1: Insurability and Risk Selection")
- Paste EVERYTHING from ExamFX - even if it looks bad or incomplete, I need it all
- Don't worry about formatting - just dump it in, I'll clean it up
- If ExamFX has quiz questions at the end, include those too

## What I Do With Your Content

For every concept in your raw markdown, I will:

1. **Rewrite it in plain English** - 6th grade reading level, no jargon without explanation
2. **Add real-world examples** - Every single concept gets a "The Setup / What Happens / The Result" scenario
3. **Create visual aids** - Comparison grids, color-coded boxes, flow diagrams, key number highlights
4. **Add Exam Trap alerts** - Common tricks and mistakes to watch for
5. **Build a Cheat Sheet** - Condensed reference at the bottom of each part
6. **Include "5 Things You MUST Know"** - Quick summary at the top of every part
